## Elevator Pitch by Segment

### Adaptive Sports Programs
RAINIER is an adaptive all-electric trail trike designed to help programs serve more riders — with a high seating position for easy transfers, three adjustable rider positions, and a price point that makes fleet adoption realistic. We're selecting 3–5 founding partners to receive early units and shape how the platform develops.

### Faith Camps (Jewish, Christian, etc.)
RAINIER opens trail riding to campers who would otherwise be excluded — without requiring dedicated staff expertise or complex setup. One volunteer, under 10 minutes, and a rider who's never been on an adaptive bike before can be out on the trail. We're looking for camps that want to expand what's possible for their participants.

### Rental Programs
RAINIER is built for repeat multi-rider use — standardized fasteners, standard bike shop serviceability, and a seating system that adjusts between riders in 20–30 minutes. Fleet discount available at 2+ units. We're working with early rental partners to validate the operational model and refine the platform.

## Key Talking Points

- **Weight:** Under 64 lbs — light enough for one volunteer to load and unload
- **Range:** 40–80 miles on a single charge
- **Price:** $8k initial retail; 3-year target of $5k
- **Fleet discount** available at 2+ units
- **Single rear wheel** allows closer rider transfer than quad-style competitors
- **Two fastener sizes (M5 and M8)** — any bike shop can service it
- **Three rider positions** — 90° seated, 45° forward, long-sitting
- **No dedicated trailer needed** — fits existing transport solutions

## Common Objections

**"We already have adaptive equipment."**
RAINIER is designed to complement existing fleets, not replace them. The focus on easy transfers and single-volunteer setup makes it practical to add without significant operational overhead.

**"We can't afford it."**
Fleet discount at 2+ units. The LOI is non-binding and free — it just gets you to the front of the line and opens a conversation about timing and quantity.

**"It's not proven yet."**
Gen-1 prototype has been field tested in Whistler (BC), Bentonville/Ozarks (AR), and Tiger Mountain (WA). We're selecting founding partners specifically to broaden that validation across more rider populations and program types.

**"We need to see it first."**
Send them the demo page with a tracked UTM link and follow up after. Demo units are being produced domestically for early partners.
