Product

Design Overview

Core product priorities and component intent. Click a card to expand, hover the diagram to inspect.

Rider Adaptability

  • Three rider positions from upright to long-sitting.
  • Seat, support, and restraint points adjust for individual fit.
  • Focus on inclusive rider onboarding.

Fleet Operation

  • Single-volunteer setup objective.
  • Compatible with existing transport solutions.
  • Fast transition between riders.

Maintenance Simplicity

  • Standard bike shop service strategy.
  • Low custom part count.
  • Two core fastener sizes reduce tool management.

Cost Accessibility

  • Target purchase range supports fleet adoption planning.
  • Low seasonal consumables target.
  • Early pilot feedback intended to stabilize long-term service cost.

Trail Performance

  • Tadpole stance for predictable stability.
  • Rigid frame prioritizes reliability.
  • Hub motor supports climbing and assist needs.

    Rider Limits

    Max height: 6'2" Max weight: 220 lbs Volunteer adjustment: 20–30 min
    Position 1

    90-degree seated with foot pegs

    • Upright seated posture for stable trunk support.
    • Foot pegs position lower legs in controlled neutral placement.
    • Uses stock bike pedals for personal user fit preference (straps, studs, etc).
    Position 2

    45-degree forward position with foot pads

    • Useful for shorter legs or for riders who prefer their knees closer to their chest for stabilization.
    • Foot pads support broader lower-limb contact area.
    Position 3

    Long-sitting configuration with adjustable leg supports

    • Great for riders already used to standard adaptive off-road seating interfaces.
    • Leg supports provide extended lower-limb guidance.
    • Wide adjustability accommodates anthropometric diversity.

    Pilot Program Partnership

    RAINIER is designed to help adaptive programs serve more riders — with easier transfers, simpler volunteer workflows, and a price point that makes fleet adoption realistic. We're selecting 3–5 pilot partners to receive early units and shape how the platform develops.

    Pilot partners receive:
    • Priority allocation in the first production run
    • Direct input into design refinement based on your program's rider population and operational needs
    • A dedicated point of contact through delivery and early deployment
    What signing an LOI means

    Our Letter of Intent is non-binding. It expresses mutual interest in moving forward and opens a direct conversation about delivery timing, quantity, and program fit. Either party can withdraw at any time before a final agreement is signed — no penalties, no obligations. If RAINIER fits your program, this is the lowest-friction way to stay at the front of the line.
